--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2g. THE CONFIDENCE PARADOX IN HARMFUL PERSUASION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  In harmful persuasion cases (human changed from correct → incorrect):
    · 17/32 (53.1%) became MORE confident after switching to wrong answer
    · 15/32 (46.9%) stayed same or became LESS confident

  Interpretation: Many harmful persuasion cases are low-confidence switches —
  the human was already uncertain (mean initial belief=0.48),
  the AI provided a plausible-sounding but wrong rationale, and the human
  hedged by switching without a strong conviction boost. This is distinct from
  the high-confidence harmful flips, where the AI actively convinced the human.

  Initial vs. final confidence in harmful cases:
    step1 (initial): n=32  mean=+0.476  median=+0.500  stdev=0.255  min=+0.000  max=+0.940
    step2 (post-AI): n=32  mean=+0.562  median=+0.570  stdev=0.268  min=+0.020  max=+1.000

  Initial vs. final confidence in helpful cases:
    step1 (initial): n=143  mean=+0.405  median=+0.500  stdev=0.261  min=+0.000  max=+1.000
    step2 (post-AI): n=143  mean=+0.613  median=+0.590  stdev=0.264  min=+0.000  max=+1.000
